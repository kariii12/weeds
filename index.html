Wood Avens Dicophar YES Spray normally. Highly susceptible to Dicophar’s multi-mix.
Herb Robert Dicophar YES Soft annual weed. Dies off very quickly from a standard spray.
Spiny Sowthistle Dicophar YES Hits the deep central taproot. Spray center of the weed directly.
Common Sowthistle Dicophar YES Treat identical to spiny sowthistle.
Fringed Willowherb Dicophar YES Catch it early before it develops its wind-blown fluffy seeds.
Fox and Cubs Dicophar YES Hairy Leaf Warning: The hairs block liquid. Spray on a warm, sunny day when the plant pores are wide open for maximum absorption.
Creeping Buttercup Dicophar YES Thoroughly wet the leaves to stop the creeping surface runners.
Dandelion Dicophar YES Destroys the central core. May take 2 weeks to fully rot the taproot.
Cleavers (Sticky Jack) Dicophar YES Spray while small. If left to climb hedges, it becomes hard to trace.
Wavy Bittercress Dicophar YES Spray quickly. If you step on it when mature, it violently shoots seeds.
Common Daisy Dicophar YES Systemic clean-out. Great for melting dense daisy mats out of lawns.
Spear Thistle Dicophar YES Fully susceptible. Spray the crown of the thistle generously.
Smooth Cat's Ear Dicophar YES Looks like a flat dandelion; treated exactly the same way.
Thyme-leaved Speedwell Dicophar YES Tricky Waxy Leaf: Speedwell is tiny and waxy. Use a very fine mist setting on your sprayer so the droplets don't bounce off.
Common Dog-Violet Dicophar YES Easily handled via standard blanket or spot-sprayer batch.
Orchard Grass (Cocksfoot) Gallup XL NO Weed Grass: Paint neat or 1-to-1 diluted Gallup directly onto blades with a brush. Do not spray or it will leave a dead circle of lawn.
Rough Bluegrass Gallup XL NO Weed Grass: Paint target blades carefully. Will ignore lawn weedkiller.
Perennial Ryegrass Gallup XL NO Weed Grass: Only target if it is clumping heavily and ruining finer lawn grass strains. Paint individual clumps.
Siberian Squill Gallup XL NO Tough Bulb: Fleshy leaves. Cut the foliage back slightly, then brush pure Gallup onto the open, bleeding stems.
Spanish Bluebell Gallup XL NO Waxy Bulb Warning: Step on and bruise the leaves first to crack the gloss coat, add a drop of Fairy Liquid to your Gallup cup, then paint.
Bittersweet (Nightshade) Gallup XL NO Woody Vine: Toxic. Paint leaves carefully, or cut low and brush Gallup straight onto the fresh stem wound.
Brambles Gallup XL NO Woody Brush: Paint leaves with a paintbrush. Avoid letting any brush drips hit your green lawn underneath.
Ivy Gallup XL NO Waxy Leaf Warning: Crush/scratch the glossy leaves with your shoe or gloved hand first, then paint Gallup over the scratch marks.
California Privet Gallup XL NO Stump Treatment: Cut down to a flat stump. Paint 100% pure undiluted Gallup onto the fresh cut ring within 15 minutes.
Sweet Mock-Orange Gallup XL NO Stump Treatment: Cut down and paint raw wood with pure Gallup.
Elder Gallup XL NO Stump Treatment: Cut down. For thick stumps, drill 2-3 downward holes into the top outer ring and pour neat Gallup directly inside.
Franchet's Cotoneaster Gallup XL NO Woody Shrub: Glossy leaves. Best killed by cutting it down to the base and painting the raw stump immediately.
