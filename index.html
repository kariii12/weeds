3 Quick Things to Remember for Application

1. The Soap Trick

When mixing your cup of Gallup for Ivy, Brambles, and Spanish Bluebells, adding a single drop of Fairy Liquid is critical. It breaks the liquid surface tension so the chemical sticks cleanly to waxy coats instead of rolling off harmlessly.

2. Sprayer Math for Dicophar

To fill your pressure sprayer for general broadleaf weeds on the turf, mix exactly 7.5ml of Dicophar concentrate per 1 Litre of water. This gives you maximum strength while keeping the grass perfectly safe.

3. The "Fresh Cut" Rule

For tree stumps and woody shrubs (Elder, Privet, Mock-Orange), you must paint pure, undiluted Gallup onto the cut wood surface within 15 minutes of sawing it down. Waiting longer allows the tree sap to seal the wound, locking out the chemical.
